vessel x storage- jar

This project has been about identity. Through these sesssions, we are experimenting between photography, photoshop, coding- which are all aspects of media studies x photography. I have enjoyed looking around the Korea exibihtion and taking 360 degrees photos of our object that we chose. Becasue of how big the British Museum is, I thought it was going to be enjoyable yet educational.

My object I chose was my favourite CD and the object I chose from the museum was a storage jar, which was passed down generations. The album art of the CD included two grandfathers, so I linked the two together with age/ generation. The colour scheme of the jar was a royal blue on a white jar. The theme of the CD was white with blues and greys. They fitted in together quite well physically. I liked both of them for their meaning and underlining mention of importance of family and treasuring what you have, instead of grieveing of what you lack.

The British Museum has changed from what i have remembered when I was younger, it has become more advance in its technology.It is a lot more involved with students of all ages. Many Primary school's take advantage of the museum and it's reachability.
